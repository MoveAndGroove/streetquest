3. Anonymized Data Sharing

We share only anonymized, aggregated data — never individual user data. For example: "500 users walked down Broad Street last week" — never "Jen walked down Broad Street on Tuesday."

Anonymized and aggregated data may be shared with:

  • Local businesses: foot traffic patterns near their location (e.g. "200 Street Quest users pass your block daily")
  • City planning organizations: walking pattern data to improve urban infrastructure
  • Tourism boards: aggregated data about popular walking routes and destinations
  • Research institutions: anonymized urban mobility data for academic research
  • Brand partners: aggregate data about active walker demographics (no individual data)

All shared data is stripped of any information that could identify individual users before sharing. We use industry-standard anonymization techniques including data aggregation, generalization, and noise addition.
